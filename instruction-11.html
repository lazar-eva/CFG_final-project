Creamy Vegan Harissa Pasta

This is pasta is packed with flavour, one of which is called 'harrisa'. Harissa is a paste consisting of a variety of chili peppers, spices, and herbs and is native to the Maghreb. As seen in the video accompanying this recipe, when following the directions, this dish can come together in as little as 15 minutes. Dinner will be ready before you know it!

Ingredients

  • 8.8 oz (250 g) dry linguine noodles
  • 2 tsp (10 mL) olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 roasted red bell peppers from a jar, chopped
  • 2 cups (60 g) fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1½ Tbsp (25 g) canned tomato paste
  • 1 vegetable bouillon cube, crushed
  • 2 Tbsp (30 mL) harissa sauce
  • 1 Tbsp (3 g) dried oregano
  • ½ tsp paprika powder
  • ¼ tsp salt, plus more to taste
  • 1 cup (240 mL) soy cooking cream
  • 1 cup (134 g) frozen green peas
  • 3 Tbsp (7 g) nutritional yeast flakes
  • 3 Tbsp (25 g) toasted pine nuts
  • ½ lemon, zested, juiced

Directions

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions, draining it 1 minute before it's al dente. Just before draining, reserve ½ cup (120 mL) of the pasta cooking water.
  2. To a sauté pan on high heat, add the olive oil. While the oil heats up, chop the onion and add it to the pan to cook for 3 - 5 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, mince the garlic, and chop the roasted red bell peppers and spinach.
  4. Add the garlic to the pan to cook for 2 minutes, stirring often. When lightly golden, add in the tomato paste, bouillon cube, harissa, oregano, paprika powder, and salt, and cook this for another 2 minutes.
  5. Add the reserved pasta cooking water to the pan, then drain the pasta. Add the drained pasta to the pot as well, along with all the remaining ingredients: the cooking cream, peas, nutritional yeast, pine nuts, bell pepper, and spinach.
  6. Once heated through, add some lemon zest (optional) and squeeze over the lemon juice. Serve with fresh basil and enjoy!

Notes

Harissa offers a deep tangy and spicy flavour, so add less if you'd prefer less heat.
