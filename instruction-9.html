Harissa Spiced Beans

This recipe is suitable for both breakfast and dinner. The butter beans and sweet cherry tomatoes are coated in a slightly spicy harissa sauce and served on a bed of tangy coconut yogurt alongside some warm, toasted pita bread. This recipe is a journey through mouth-watering flavours!

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp (10 mL) olive oil
  • 1 small red onion, sliced
  • 1 clove garlic, crushed
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, chopped
  • 1½ cups (255 g) cooked butter beans
  • 10 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 Tbsp (30 mL) harissa sauce, more to taste
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ⅛ tsp ground black pepper
  • ½ lemon, juiced
  • ½ cup (120 mL) unsweetened coconut yogurt
  • ½ avocado, sliced (optional)
  • 2 toasted pita breads

Directions

  1. Heat a large pan on medium-high heat and add the oil, onion, garlic, and bell pepper. Cook for 3 - 4 minutes, until softened.
  2. Add the beans, tomatoes, and harissa, and cook for 3 - 4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the tomatoes have softened a bit. Deglaze the pan with a small splash of water if necessary.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, and stir in the lemon juice.
  4. Divide the coconut yogurt between your plates, top with the harissa beans, some avocado slices, and serve with toasted pita. Enjoy!

Notes

The beans can be stored separately from the other components in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days.
